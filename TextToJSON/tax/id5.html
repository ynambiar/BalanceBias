Hawaii Languishes at Bottom of US Tax System

24 Sep, 2015

By: Jordan

1809 2
Tweet

Hawaii has ranked poorly in the 2015 report brought out by WalletHub. The website dedicated to personal finance has published its yearly “Most & Least Fair State Tax Systems” and the state flounders at the bottom ranks. It fares slightly better than Washington and Georgia among the 50 states.

Problem of defining “fair” state 

The diversity of taxation policies in the United States has compelled the report publishers to delve into local and state tax systems. It aims to find out which is regarded as the best by the residents. According to the website, the results of this national survey examined what Americans believe is a “fair” state. The idea of a local tax system is also obtained. The findings are then ranked by analysts. The rankings are dependent on how closely public perception matches actual tax systems.

One important finding is that the bottom 20 percent or the poor are overtaxed to the maximum limit in Illinois, Hawaii and Washington. It is one of top  unfair 10 states where middle class citizens, comprising about 40 percent are forced to pay the maximum tax too. The present local and state tax systems are mostly regarded as unfair, as per the report. Even as a majority of Americans think that progressive tax systems are fair, almost every US state has regressive local and state tax structures.

Other than the survey data, a number of other sources were also used to create rankings. These sources were gleaned from US Census Bureau and Institute of Taxation and Economic Policy. 

Best tax states

When it comes to fair tax states, South Carolina occupies third place. The WalletHub website came to this conclusion after considering the different tax systems imposed by different states. The result was made after analyzing all the 50 states and examining how they tax the poor compared to the rich.

South Carolina was beaten by Oregon and Montana. It is found that the wealthiest are subjected to minimum taxation in Florida, Nevada and Wyoming. This study also revealed that a majority of Americans hold the perception that fair local and state tax systems push higher taxes when it comes to wealthier households. The survey discloses that both Republican and Democrat states under tax the wealthy and overtax the economically weaker section of society.  Conservatives support a marginally higher tax to be imposed on the poor and the wealthy to be subjected to lower tax rates.
